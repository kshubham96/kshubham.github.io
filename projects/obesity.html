Obesity Risk Analysis: Family History and Lifestyle Factors

  • Tech Stack: Excel, Weka, SQL Server.
  • Github URL: Project Link

Overview : Conducted an in-depth analysis of obesity using a dataset sourced from Kaggle. The project involved data cleansing and exploratory analysis, employing Weka and Python for data visualization and machine learning.

Summary :

  • Abstract: Explored the impact of family history on obesity risk in datasets from Mexico, Peru, and Colombia.
  • Introduction: Investigated the rising global obesity rates and the role of family history in obesity risk.
  • Method: Preprocessed data with SQL, created BMI-related variables, and employed Weka for analysis, including association rule mining and classification models.
  • Results: Discovered correlations between family history, dietary habits, alcohol intake, and obesity. Random Forest and Logistic Regression performed well in classification.
  • Discussion: Suggested early interventions for high-risk groups with a family history of obesity and lifestyle factors.
  • Conclusion: Concluded that family history is a risk factor but not the sole cause of obesity, with lifestyle choices also playing a significant role.
  • Limitations: Acknowledged limitations, including a small training dataset size and low public awareness of obesity risk factors.
